KIPS QUIZ APP

This work deals with development of android-based multiple-choice question examination system, namely: KIPS. This application is developed for educational purpose, allowing the users to prepare the multiple choice questions for different examinations conducted on provincial and national level. The main goal of the application is to enable users to practice for subjective tests conducted for admissions and recruitment, with focus on Computer science field. This quiz application includes three main modules, namely (i) computer science, (ii) verbal, and (iii) analytical. The computer science and verbal modules contains various types of sub categories. This quiz includes three functions: (i) Hint, (ii) Skip, and (iii) Pause, which are collectively named as life lines that help users to answer questions correctly. These functions can be used only once by a user. It shows progress feedback during quiz play, and at the end, the app also shows the result.

SD:  SMARTDRIVE

Road accidents are at the eight positions in causing deaths. Road accidents are concerning for commuters as well as government authorities. Earlier accident detection was based on hardware sensors that needed to be installed in the vehicle which made the technology less popular and approachable. Smartphones are readily used by most of the people today and smartphone applications are easy to install, use and maintain. SmartDrive application cover that area hence can detect road accidents without any hardware device using GPS and other sensors of smartphones. The same smartphones can also be used to alert the user of accident-prone areas which are spotted using the relatable data from smartphones themselves. The data from smartphones is filtered then clustered to find areas that are prone to accidents. The warning system will help commuter to stay alert while traveling from such areas and authorities to know which parts of roads are dangerous and need focus for reducing casualties.
